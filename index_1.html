Career-Ready Training

13-Week Cybersecurity
Beginner Training

The curriculum follows a logical progression from foundational theory to technical application — designed to take a complete beginner to job-ready in three months.

PhaseDurationFocus AreaKey Topics Covered
01 Week 1 Foundations
Core Concepts & Threat Landscape		 Cybersecurity history, terminology, motivation behind attacks, and an overview of the cyber-threat landscape.
02 Weeks 2–4 Security Principles
Risk & Defense Strategy		 Risk management, security frameworks, identifying vulnerabilities, and the trade-offs between system usability and security.
03 Weeks 5–8 Technical Skills
Applied Security Tools		 Network security, firewalls, Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), cryptography, and basic assessment commands.
04 Weeks 9–12 Specialized Practice
Offense & Defense Tactics		 Ethical hacking (intro), digital forensics, malware analysis, reverse engineering, and incident response.
05 Week 13 Capstone
Real-World Application		 Hands-on projects, simulations of cyber-investigations, and advanced threat mitigation techniques.
What This Program Covers
Concepts

Understanding that cybersecurity is not absolute — it's a balance of risk, cost, and benefit.

Tools

Exposure to industry-standard tools (e.g. Metasploit, SIEM systems) used for both offensive (penetration testing) and defensive purposes.

Best Practices

Securing systems, managing passwords and authentication (SSO, MFA), and protecting data in the cloud and on physical devices.

Emerging Trends

The application of Generative AI in cybersecurity — prompt engineering and AI-driven threat detection.

Capstone-driven by design. Every cohort ends with a real project you can show employers — not just a certificate.
